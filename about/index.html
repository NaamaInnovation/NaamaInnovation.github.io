Naama Lavee

Composer, performer, researcher of expressiveness, and dreamer.

I’m a composer, performer, art enthusiast, researcher of expressiveness, Baroque trumpet player, video editor, and dreamer.

In 2018 I graduated from Abbey Road Institute with an Advanced Diploma in Music Production and Sound Engineering, and afterwards I worked in various music venues in Berlin as well as the recording studio Idee und Klang in Basel. I later pursued higher education, earning a Bachelor’s degree in Classical Music and a Master’s degree in Composition at MTU - Cork School of Music.

In 2024 I won a composition award for The Clown March, a musical street performance, and led the multidisciplinary production Circle of Emotions. I also founded the Berceuse Ensemble, which has performed in venues including the Crawford Gallery in Cork.

I was part of the core international team that created the original circus-theatre show Marionette, where I composed and designed the sound. In addition, I perform regularly with the Baroque trumpet group at Dublin Castle as part of the annual Dublin Handel Festival.
